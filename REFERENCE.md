# TalkPrep AI Reference Guide

This document provides detailed reference information for the TalkPrep AI skill.

## Time Allocation Guidelines

### By Talk Duration

| Duration | Intro | Main Content | Conclusion | Q&A |
|----------|-------|--------------|------------|-----|
| 5 min    | 10%   | 75%          | 15%        | 0%  |
| 15 min   | 10%   | 70%          | 10%        | 10% |
| 30 min   | 10%   | 65%          | 10%        | 15% |
| 45 min   | 10%   | 60%          | 10%        | 20% |
| 60+ min  | 8%    | 62%          | 10%        | 20% |

### Words Per Minute Guidelines

| Tone | Words/Minute | Notes |
|------|--------------|-------|
| Formal | 120 | Slower, more deliberate |
| Conversational | 150 | Natural pace |
| Energetic | 170 | Fast but clear |

## Slide Guidelines

### Slides Per Minute
- Target: ~1 slide per minute
- Lightning talks: 1 slide per 30 seconds
- Technical demos: Allow 2-3 minutes per demo slide

### Content Limits
- Maximum bullet points: 5 (prefer 3-4)
- Maximum words per bullet: 10
- Maximum words per slide: 40

### Slide Layouts

| Layout | Use When |
|--------|----------|
| Title | Opening and closing slides |
| Section Header | Major topic transitions |
| Bullet Points | Listing key points |
| Two Column | Comparisons, pros/cons |
| Code | Technical demonstrations |
| Quote | Expert opinions, inspiration |
| Image Full | Visual impact, demos |
| Timeline | Process flows, history |

## Opening Hook Strategies

### By Audience Type

**Technical:**
- Show the end result first
- Share a production war story
- Present surprising benchmarks
- Demo a common mistake

**Academic:**
- Present a research question
- Show unexpected data
- Reference seminal work
- Identify a knowledge gap

**Business:**
- Lead with ROI numbers
- Share a competitor case study
- Present market trends
- Show customer impact

**General:**
- Tell a personal story
- Ask a relatable question
- Share a surprising fact
- Use a familiar analogy

## Transition Phrases

### Moving Between Sections
- "Now that we've covered X, let's dive into Y."
- "This brings us to an important point: Y."
- "Building on what we just discussed..."
- "With that foundation in place..."
- "Here's where it gets interesting."

### Bridging to Conclusion
- "So what does this all mean?"
- "Let me tie this together..."
- "The key takeaway is..."
- "If you remember nothing else..."

## Q&A Question Categories

### Technical Questions
- Implementation details
- Performance characteristics
- Edge cases and limitations
- Alternative approaches

### Clarification Questions
- "Can you explain X again?"
- "What did you mean by Y?"
- "How does that relate to Z?"

### Challenge Questions
- Questioning assumptions
- Presenting counterarguments
- Asking about failures
- Probing limitations

### Expansion Questions
- Requests for more depth
- Related topics
- Future directions
- Resources for learning

### Practical Questions
- Implementation guidance
- Resource requirements
- Getting started steps
- Team/organization adoption

## Redirect Strategies for Difficult Questions

1. **Bridge**: "That's interesting, and what's even more relevant is..."
2. **Defer**: "Great question. Let me address that after, as it deserves full attention."
3. **Involve Audience**: "Has anyone else encountered this?"
4. **Partial Answer**: "I can speak to part of that..."
5. **Honest Boundary**: "That's outside my expertise, but I can point you to resources."
6. **Reframe**: "If I understand correctly, you're asking about..."

## Practice Recommendations

### General
- Record yourself and review
- Practice in front of a mirror
- Do 3+ full run-throughs
- Practice with time constraints

### By Talk Type

**Keynote:**
- Perfect your opening (10x practice)
- Work on dramatic pauses
- Rehearse call to action

**Technical:**
- Test all demos multiple times
- Prepare backup slides
- Practice explaining simply

**Workshop:**
- Time each exercise
- Prepare for common errors
- Have extension activities ready

**Lightning:**
- Time to the second
- Cut ruthlessly
- Practice transitions

**Panel:**
- Prepare talking points, not scripts
- Practice active listening
- Prepare data for debates

- Machine-readable

## Accessibility Considerations

### Visual
- High contrast text
- Large, readable fonts
- Don't rely on color alone
- Describe images verbally

### Cognitive
- One idea per slide
- Clear hierarchy
- Consistent formatting
- Allow processing time

### Auditory
- Provide written materials
- Use visual cues for key points
- Repeat important information
